Stephen Hawking

Dr. Stephen William Hawking (* 8 January 1942 in Oxford, England; â€  14 March 2018 in Cambridge, England) was an English theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author who was director of research at the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Cambridge at the time of his death.

He is one of the most well known physicists of all time, with his scientific works including a collaboration work on gravitational singularity theorems in the framework of general relativity, the prediction of black holes emitting radiation (now often called Hawking Radiation), the Black hole information paradox and much, much more.
He also achieved great commercial success with his books like A Brief History of Time, which appeared on the Sunday Times bestseller list for a record-breaking 237 weeks, and received numerous honors, for example:

  • Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • Fellow of the Royal Society
  • Lifetime member of the Pontifical Academy of Sciences

Here is a quick rundown of his life:

  • 1942 - Born in Oxford, England

  • 1950s - Receiving primary and secondary education

  • 1959 - Beginning his university education (at the age of 17)

  • 1962 - Beginning his graduate work at Cambridge

  • 1966 - Obtained his PhD in applied mathematics & theoretical physics

  • late 1960s - early 1970s - Serious career work with multiple publishes

  • 1970s - Researching black holes

  • 1980s - Reseraching the beginning of the universe

  • 1988 - Publishment of his most famous book, A Brief History of Time

  • 1990s - Continuing his work; writing more books such as Universe: The Cosmos Explained or On the Shoulders of giants

  • 2004 - Claimed he solved his own Black Hole information paradox (still disputed unter physicists)

  • 2007 - Founded the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology

  • 2013 - Won one of the two Breakthrough Prizes in Fundamental Physics for his discovery of Hawking radiation

  • 2018 - Peaceful death at his home in Cambridge
